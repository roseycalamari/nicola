My Tennis Journey

Key moments on my path to becoming a professional player

2010

First Steps

Started playing tennis at the age of 6 years. Playing just for fun with my dad in seedorf (Uri).

"I had so much fun playing tennis with my dad after his work or at the weekend"

2014

Junior Interclub

At the age of 10 I Started competing in junior interclub tournaments. This was my first taste of competitive tennis and team play.

"Playing my first matches with my cousin and a friend of mine was so much fun and it was a great time for me."

2023

Professional Debut

At the age of 19 I made my professional debut at the ITF Mens in Metzingen (GER). A big step in my tennis career, competing against professional players.

"My first pro match was nerve-wracking but amazing."

2025

ITF Circuit

Currently playing ITF tournaments, working hard to improve my ranking and gain more experience.

"Every tournament teaches me something new."

Life outside of the tennis court

Balancing my professional career with other passions

I believe that maintaining interests outside of tennis helps me stay mentally fresh and brings new perspectives to my professional career. These activities reconnect me with nature and provide valuable contrast to the structured competitive environment of tennis.

Windsurfing

When I'm not on the tennis court, you'll often find me on the water. Windsurfing provides me with both physical challenges and mental relaxation. The Swiss lakes offer beautiful settings to practice this sport that I've enjoyed since my teenage years.

Academic Work

Beyond tennis, I work as an IT engineer at GIPO, where I specialize in assembling and configuring the digital components of machines. This role allows me to apply my technical skills and problem-solving abilities in a professional setting, complementing my athletic career with valuable industry experience.
